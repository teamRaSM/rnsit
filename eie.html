ELECTRONICS AND INSTRUMENTATION ENGINEERINGC

Dr. Andhe Pallavi

Professor & Head

The department of Electronics & Instrumentation Engg is headed by Dr. Andhe Pallavi. The department is existing right from the inception of RNSIT. The department has highly qualified, experienced & dedicated faculty, committed to impart technical knowledge using advanced pedagogic methodologies

Department of Electronics & Instrumentation Engg is also a recognized VTU R&D center where 05 candidates are pursuing Ph.D.

Under this Department M.Tech in Industrial Electronics is running.

The department has full-fledged labs catering to VTU curriculum & beyond. Some of our labs are Analog Electronics & IC Labs, Logic Design Lab, Instrumentation Lab, Process control Lab (incorporating PC based level, flow,temperature & pressure control loops, PLCs (GE FANUC based), LabVIEW based experiments, sensors & their signal conditioning, etc), DSP Lab (CCS & TMS320C6711 DSKs), VHDL & Verilog Lab (XC2S 200E Spartan kits), Microcontroller lab (8051 and PIC 16F877 based), Control systems Lab, Microprocessor lab (8086 based) etc. The department also features an exclusive departmental library with over 500 books donated by alumni, staff & college management.

The department has an exclusive ISOI (Instrument Society of India Students Chapter) from 2007, with a membership of over 100 students annually. IT students are also active members of IEEE and ISTE students’ chapters at RNSIT.

The department also has an excellent placement record with students being placed in companies such as Infosys. Tech-Mahindra, Wipro, Yokagawa, TCS, National Instruments, Cisco, etc. Some of our alumni have also been inducted into Indian air force, naval & army services.

The department also contributes healthily to the college’s sports team’s such basket ball, cricket, football, athletics and have won many intercollegiate & State level competitions.

The department has well equipped laboratory with sophisticated instruments. The practical classes are assisted by the well experienced lab instructor Mrs. Bhagyalakshmi. With all these strengths, the department has achieved 100% results in the past years. The department also encourages students updating their knowledge by involving them in seminars, invited talks and such activities

The Department of Physics conducted a National Conference on Phosphors and their Applications (NCPA 2010) during November 15-16, 2010. The conference had 111 participants from various parts of the country and 65 papers were presented in the conference. In the year 2011 the department conducted two events-

  1. Intercollegiate Physics Fest PHOTON 2011 on May 5th 2011
  2. National Seminar on Nuclear Technology for the Growth of the Nation on August 22, 2011.

Vision

Empowering Instrumentation Engineers to Changing Technologies.

Mission

  1. To provide knowledge in the latest technologies to make students aware of the need of measurement and control in field of engineering.
  2. To equip students with skills needed to succeed in industries and to pursue higher studies.
  3. To motivate students engage lifelong learning and contribute to society.

Regular Activities of the Instrumentation Technology department include:

  1. Release of Bi-annual students’ newsletter ‘TATVA’,
  2. An one day fest encompassing paper presentation, circuit debugging, quiz, gaming, model presentation, etc. on 20th of October every year under ISOI banner,
  3. Conducting regular training programmes for students incorporating additional curriculum (such as DSP using LabVIEW, VHDL interfacing, etc) to augment their skills, improved employment opportunities etc,
  4. Invited guest lectures and seminars from eminent industrialists & professors from elite institutions,
  5. Organizing short-term workshops & faculty development courses for in house & VTU staff of other colleges.
