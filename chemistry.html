CHEMISTRY DEPARTMENT

Dr. T. Jeevananda

Professor & Head

The Department of Chemistry at RNSIT was established in the year 2001 during the inception of the Institution. The Vision of the Department is to meet the core requirement of Chemistry in the field of Applied Science and Technology.

The Department is headed by Dr. T. Jeevananda, who has 16 years of Teaching and Research experience of which 3 years as Post Doctoral Scientist in South Korea. The other Faculty members of the department are highly qualified, experienced and dedicated teaching faculty members of which three are Doctorates and one is pursuing his Ph.D. degree. The Department is well equipped with lab facilities to meet the curriculum of Engineering Chemistry.

In the year 2008, the department got approved by VTU as R & D Centre for carrying out research work and to pursue PhD degree. The Department has a strong record of achievements both in teaching and research activities. The Department, R & D Centre has published more than 20 research articles in reputed National and International Journals in the previous years. The Department provide graduate students with the foundation needed for a meaningful professional career and a lifetime of independent learning. The department also organizes lectures to encourage students to upgrade their knowledge.

The Department of Chemistry has organized a National Conference on “Recent Trends in Materials Chemistry and Engineering (RTMCE-2011)” during September 29-30, 2011. Some of the Research Activities at the Department are,

  • Conducting Polymer Composites/Nano-Composites,
  • Material Science,
  • Electrochemistry,
  • Solid State Reactions etc.,

Laboratory

The department has well equipped laboratory with sophisticated instruments. The practical classes are assisted by the well experienced lab instructor Mrs. Bhagyalakshmi. With all these strengths, the department has achieved 100% results in the past years. The department also encourages students updating their knowledge by involving them in seminars, invited talks and such activities

The Department of Physics conducted a National Conference on Phosphors and their Applications (NCPA 2010) during November 15-16, 2010. The conference had 111 participants from various parts of the country and 65 papers were presented in the conference. In the year 2011 the department conducted two events-

  1. Intercollegiate Physics Fest PHOTON 2011 on May 5th 2011
  2. National Seminar on Nuclear Technology for the Growth of the Nation on August 22, 2011.
